Our Methodology

Key metrics used to analyze YouTube creator performance.

View Trajectory (1–7d)

Shows whether recent uploads are running hotter or cooler than the channel's usual first‑week performance. We compare each video's day‑1‑to‑7 views against the creator's typical week‑one pattern using videos from the last month only.

Interpretation: Positive values = performing above historical average, Negative values = below average, ~0% = consistent performance

Views per Upload

Summarizes the typical reach a recent upload gets by averaging total views and total uploads over the last 30 days. This gives a simple expectation for how many views new content may deliver.

Best for: Comparing creators of similar sizes without digging into deeper analytics. More reliable than individual viral hits or flops.

Audience Efficiency

Indicates how effectively subscribers convert into viewers by looking at median views on recent videos relative to the channel's subscriber base. Higher values mean the audience is actively watching.

Ranges: High (>50%) = very engaged audience, Medium (20-50%) = typical engagement, Low (<20%) = large but inactive subscriber base

Early Engagement (7d)

Captures how quickly people interact with new content by checking what share of total likes and comments arrive in the first week after publishing. Faster response suggests stronger audience resonance.

Ranges: High (>70%) = immediate audience response, Medium (40-70%) = normal timeline, Low (<40%) = engagement builds slowly
